What is GIS?

GIS stands for Geographic Information Systems. It is a technology that allows us to visualize and interact various features and their informations to see their relationship on the map.
GIS integrates databases with the locations of features (maps tied to specific known locations). Geographic Information System (GIS) helps people visualize and create information that can be used to make decisions and solve problems. A geographic information system (GIS) lets us visualize, question, analyze, and interpret data to understand relationships, patterns, and trends of features and events. GIS benefits organizations of all sizes and in almost every industry.

A GIS (geographic or geospatial information system) allows us to record the information and show the location of anything(any feature) available on the earth(longitude or latitude) and then to add additional information of other features. The GIS then allows the individual layers, or themes as they are called to be linked. Analysis of the information can then be undertaken using the statistical and analytical tools that are provided as part of the GIS. By providing spatial analysis of suitably coded data it is possible to provide striking, visual representations of data. These representations can often reveal patterns and trends that might otherwise have gone unnoticed without the use of GIS techniques. Visual representations of this type can also be extended further so that more considered and objective means of measurement are applied. Such approaches allow macro views to be taken in many fields. Epidemiologists are interested in the global spread of disease occurrence; criminologists are interested in local, national and international patters of criminal activity whilst geologists are interested in physical formations to predict earthquake activity and the availability of resources such as oil and gas. None of these disciplines would be able to work as they currently do without the ability to apply analytical tools to otherwise disparate sets of data in the way a Geographical Information System permits.

Introduction to GPS

This course introduce you to the Global Positioning System (GPS) and how it can be used in surveying and data collection. Emphasis is on GPS applications related to mapping, engineering and construction projects. This course is primarily aimed at the beginners surveying/engineering technicians and anyone who is planing to collect spatial data.

You wil learn about GPS surveying equipment, setting GPS, Data collection procedures, upload and using GPS data into GIS system.
